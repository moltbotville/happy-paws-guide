# Best Dog Food for Dogs With Sensitive Stomachs

## Understanding Sensitive Stomachs in Dogs

Dogs can develop sensitive stomachs for a variety of reasons, including food intolerances, allergies, digestive disorders, or simply a sensitive GI tract. Common symptoms of a sensitive stomach in dogs include:

- Frequent vomiting or diarrhea
- Excessive gas or bloating
- Lack of appetite
- Weight loss
- Skin irritation or hot spots

Choosing the right dog food is crucial for managing a sensitive stomach and keeping your pup happy and healthy. In this article, we'll explore the best dog food options for dogs with sensitive stomachs.

## FAQs About Dog Food for Sensitive Stomachs

### 1. What ingredients should I avoid in dog food for sensitive stomachs?
Common ingredients to avoid include grains (wheat, corn, soy), artificial additives, and highly processed proteins. Look for limited-ingredient formulas with a single source of high-quality protein.

### 2. How can I transition my dog to a new sensitive stomach dog food?
When switching your dog's food, do so gradually over the course of 7-10 days. This will allow your pup's digestive system to adjust to the new formula. Mix in the new food slowly, starting with a 25% new to 75% old ratio, and gradually increase the new food percentage.

### 3. Are there any natural remedies to help soothe a sensitive stomach in dogs?
In addition to a specialized dog food, probiotics, digestive enzymes, and bone broth can all help support healthy digestion in dogs with sensitive stomachs.
